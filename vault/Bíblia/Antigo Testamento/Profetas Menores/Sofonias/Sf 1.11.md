It may have been a quarter of Jerusalem devoted to minting operations. It may have been the “Phoenician quarter” of the city where foreign merchants resided. Others think it is a valley, so named here for its mortar-like shape. Some Jewish authorities think it is a reference to the Kedron valley. all the merchant. Ne 3:31, 32. Ho ✓12:7, 8. Jn *2:16.
