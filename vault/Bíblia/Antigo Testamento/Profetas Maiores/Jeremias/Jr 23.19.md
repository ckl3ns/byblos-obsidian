## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`a whirlwind. As whirlwinds are sometimes fatal to travelers, who overwhelmed by them in the desert, they are elegantly employed to denote the certainty, as well as suddenness, of the destruction of the wicked. Mr. Morier (Second Journey, p. 202), describing the whirlwinds of Persia, says they swept along the country in different directions, in a manner truly terrific, carrying away in their vortex sand, branches, and the stubble of the fields, and really appearing to make a communication between the earth and the clouds. Je 4:11. 25:32. 30:23. Ps *58:9. Pr *1:24-27. 10:25. Is 5:25-28. 21:1. 40:24. 66:15, 16. Am 1:14. Na 1:3-6. Zc 9:14.`
