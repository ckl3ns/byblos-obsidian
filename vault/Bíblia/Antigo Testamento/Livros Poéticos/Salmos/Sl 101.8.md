## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`early. lit. at morning. ƒ171T4, Jb +7:18. By Synecdoche of the Part, “morning” is put for a more lengthened period or continuous time. Here it should not be rendered “early,” as in the A.V., nor “morning by morning,” as in the R.V., as though in millennial days each morning would commence with, and each day begin with, executions! It means more than that. It means continually; so that all through the Millennium all workers of iniquity will be continually cut off (B655, 656). Ps +*59:16. *90:14. Is +*17:14. Je *21:12.
