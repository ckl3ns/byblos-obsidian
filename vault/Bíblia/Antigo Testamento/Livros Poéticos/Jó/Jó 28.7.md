## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`a path. ver. 21-23. Jb 11:6. 38:19, 24. Ro 11:33. the vulture’s eye. Ayah, rendered the kite in Le 11:14 and vulture in Dt 14:13, is supposed by Bochart (vol. iii. p. 192) to be the bird called by the Arabians juju, from its note, and which the ancients named aesalon, i.e. the merlin, a bird celebrated for its sharp-sightedness.`
