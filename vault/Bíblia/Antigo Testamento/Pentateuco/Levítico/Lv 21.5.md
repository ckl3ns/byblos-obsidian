## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`not make baldness. This custom is also called ‘rounding the corners of the head’ (Le 19:27), and seems to have been performed in honor of some idol. Herodotus observes (l. iii. c. 8) that the Arabs shave, or cut their hair round, in honor of Bacchus, who, they say, had his hair cut in this way. He also says (l. iv. c. 175) that the Macians, a people of Lybia, cut their hair round, so as to leave a tuft on the top of the head. In this way the Chinese still cut their hair. The hair was much used in divination among the ancients: and for purposes of religious superstition among the Greeks (See Homer, Iliad, xxiii. v. 142. and Virgil, Aenead, iv. 698). Le 10:6. 19:27, 28. Dt 14:1. Is 15:2. 22:12. Je 9:26n. 16:6. 41:5. 48:37. Ezk 44:20. Am 8:10. Mi 1:16.`
