## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`the tabernacle with ten curtains. The word mishcan, here rendered tabernacle, from shachan, to dwell, means simply a dwelling-place or habitation (❅S#4908h, Ex +25:9n). “When God had brought the children of Israel out of Egypt,” says the very learned Dr. Cudworth, “resolving to manifest himself in a peculiar manner present among them, he thought good to dwell amongst them in a visible and external manner; and therefore, while they were in the wilderness, and sojourned in tents, he would have a tent or tabernacle built, to sojourn with them also. Now, the tabernacle being thus a house for God to dwell in visibly, to make up the notion of dwelling or habitation complete, there must be all things suitable to a house belonging to it. Hence, in the holy place, there must be a table and a candlestick, because these were the ordinary furniture of a room. The table must have its dishes, and spoons, and bowls, and covers, belonging to it, though they were never used; and always be furnished with bread upon it. The candlestick must have its lamps continually burning,” etc. Ex 16:16 (◐S#168h). 25:8, 9n. 27:21n. ◐33:7n. *36:8-19. 40:◐2n, 17-19, 33n. 1 Ch 17:1. 21:29. Jn =1:14. 2:21. He 8:2. 9:9, 23, 24. Re 21:3. fine twined linen. ver. 36. Ex 25:4. 35:6, 35. Re 19:8. cherubims. See on Ex 25:18. cunning work. Heb. the work of a cunning workman, or, embroiderer. ver. 31. Ex 28:6, 15. *36:8, 35. 39:3, 8.`
