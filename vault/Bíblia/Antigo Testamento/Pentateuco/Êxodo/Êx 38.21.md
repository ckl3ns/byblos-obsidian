The word tabernacle is used in many different senses, and signifies (1) A tent or pavilion, Nu 24:5; Mt 17:4. (2) A house or dwelling, Jb 11:4; 22:23. (3) A kind of tent, which is designated, to speak after the manner of men, the palace of the Most High, the dwelling of the God of Israel, Ex 26:1; He 9:2, 3. (4) Christ’s human nature, of which the Jewish tabernacle was a type, wherein God dwells really, substantially, and personally, He 8:2; 9:11. (5) The true church militant, Ps 15:1. (6) Our natural body, in which the soul lodges as in a tabernacle, 2 Co 5:1; 2 P 1:13. (7) The token of God’s gracious presence, Re 21:3. by the hand. Nu 4:28-33. Ezr 8:26-30. Ithamar.
