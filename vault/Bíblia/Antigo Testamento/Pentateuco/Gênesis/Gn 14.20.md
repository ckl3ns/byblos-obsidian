Later, under Mosaic law, the obligation of the Jew in Israel involved the payment of several tithes amounting to about one quarter of his income. It appears that the first tithe was paid for the support of the Levites (Le 27:30-33. Nu 18:21-32). The second tithe was to be applied to festival purposes (Dt 12:5-7, 17. 14:22-29). Scholars differ in their opinion as to whether the third tithe was a substitute for the second tithe every third year, or whether it constituted a tithe in addition to the first two.
