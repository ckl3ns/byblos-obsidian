Jg 2:10. 1 K 2:10. 11:43. Ac 13:36. The expression ‘was gathered to his people’ does not relate to burial, for this was not so: Abraham’s “people” dwelt at this time in Haran, and he was buried at Hebron. Besides which, the fact of burial is here, and in many other places, specified over and above (see Ge 15:15. 35:29. 1 K 2:10. 11:43, etc.). Nor is it a mere synonym for dying: for in many places, as here, it is specified over and above the fact, here repeatedly expressed, of death (see ver. 17. Ge 35:29. 49:33. Nu 20:26. Dt 32:50). The only assignable sense, therefore, is that of reference to a state of further personal existence beyond death: and the expression thus forms a remarkable testimony to the O.T. belief in a future state (Henry Alford, The Book of Genesis and Part of the Book of Exodus, p. 111-112).`
