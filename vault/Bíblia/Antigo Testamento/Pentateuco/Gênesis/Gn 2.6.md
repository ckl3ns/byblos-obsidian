## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`there went up a mist. or, a mist which went up. Ge ◐*2:5. ◐*7:4. Jb 37:6. Ps 135:7. 148:8. Je 10:12, 13. ƒ63I1C1. Ellipsis of omitted negative particle from the preceding clause B93. Here, “mist” may be rendered “no mist” if three negative reasons why plants and herbs of the field did not grow are intended: (1) no rain; (2) no man; (3) no mist to water the ground. By the figure Ellipsis of repetition where an omitted negative particle is to be repeated from the preceding clause, the last of two or three negatives are not necessary and must be supplied, as in Dt 33:6. 1 S 2:3. 1 K +2:9n. Ps *9:18. 38:1. 75:5. Pr 24:12. 25:27. Is 38:18. face. Ge +1:2.`
