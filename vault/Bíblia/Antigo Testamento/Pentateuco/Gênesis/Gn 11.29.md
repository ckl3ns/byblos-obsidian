## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`Nahor. Ge 24:10, 15. 29:5. 31:53. Jsh 24:2. 1 Ch 1:26. Sarai. Ge 17:15. 20:12. 1 P 3:6. Milcah. i.e. queen; counsel, ❅S#4435h. Ge 22:20, 23. 24:15, 24, 47. Nu 26:33. 27:1. 36:11. Jsh 17:3. (1) A daughter of Haran. She was the wife of Nahor, a brother of Abraham. (2) A daughter of Zelophehad, Nu 26:33; Jsh 17:3. Iscah. i.e. observant; she looks abroad, ❅S#3252h. Iscah is called the daughter-in-law of Terah, (ver. 31,) as being Abram’s wife; yet Abram afterwards said, “she is the daughter of my father, but not the daughter of my mother.” (Ge 20:12.) Probably Haran was the eldest son of Terah, and Abram his youngest by another wife: and thus Sarai was the daughter, or grand-daughter of Terah, Abram’s father, but not of his mother.`
