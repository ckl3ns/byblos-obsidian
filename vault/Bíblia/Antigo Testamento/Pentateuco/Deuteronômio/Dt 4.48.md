## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`Aroer. Dt 2:36. 3:12. Jsh 13:24, etc. even unto. The Samaritan interpreter has, “unto the mountain of snow, which is Hermon.” The Chaldee Targumist, who places it at Caesarea, and Samaritan interpreter, call it “the mountain of snow,” because of its being always covered with snow; and Jerome informs us that in the summer time snow used to be carried from thence to Tyre. See on Dt 3:9. Sion. i.e. lofty; peak, ❅S#7865h, only here. Dt 3:9, Shenir. Ps 133:3. (1) One of the names of Mount Hermon, Dt 4:48. (2) The Greek form of Zion, designating a district of Jerusalem, Ps 65:1; Mt 21:5; Jn 12:15; Re +14:1. Hermon. Ps 133:3.`
