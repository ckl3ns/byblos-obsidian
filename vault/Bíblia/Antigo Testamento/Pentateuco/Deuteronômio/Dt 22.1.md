`Thou shalt. Ex 23:4. Ezk 34:4, 16. Mt 10:6. 15:24. 18:12, 13. Lk 15:4-6. Ja 5:19, 20. 1 P 2:25. not. ƒ19. Anastrophe; or, Arraignment B699: the position of one word changed so as to be set over against the other; the inversion or reversal of words. The word thus put out of its usual place receives great emphasis. Here, the negative is put with “see” instead of with “hide,” in order to emphasize the command, which would otherwise tamely read “If thou shalt see … thou shalt not hide,” etc. For other instances of this figure see Mi 6:10.
