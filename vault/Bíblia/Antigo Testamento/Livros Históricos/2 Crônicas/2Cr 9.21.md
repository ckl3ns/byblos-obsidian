## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`Tarshish. Bochart thinks this Tarshish was probably the promontory Cory, on the north of the island of Ceylon, which, according to him, was the land of Ophir. That it was the name of a place in the East Indies, seems probable from the articles brought thence, and also from the ships sent thither being built at Ezion-geber, on the Red sea; though Michaelis (Spicileg. Geog. Heb. Exter. p. 98, etc.) supposes that the fleet coasted along the shore of Africa, doubling the Cape of Good Hope, and came to Tartessus, in Spain, and thence back again the same way; that this accounts for their three years’ voyage out and home; and that Spain and the coasts of Africa furnish all the commodities which they brought back. More recently it has been determined that “ships of Tarshish” or “Tarshish ships” were in Solomon’s time ore-bearing ships, a refining fleet; that Solomon exchanged the copper mined in the Arabah and smelted at Ezion-geber, where Solomon had a copper smeltery set up by Phoenician craftsmen, for gold, silver, ivory, apes, baboons (1 K 9:26, 28. 10:22). Ge +10:4. See on 1 K 10:22. 22:48, Tharshish. ivory. or elephant’s teeth. peacocks. Tukkeeyim is rendered taysin in the Targum, taonon in the Alexandrian MS. of the LXX., and pavos, “peacocks,” in the Vulgate; with which the Syriac, Arabic, and Rabbins agree. Jb 39:13.`
