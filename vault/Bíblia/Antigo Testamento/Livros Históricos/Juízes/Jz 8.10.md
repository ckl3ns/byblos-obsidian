## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`Karkor. i.e. soft, level ground; battering down; excavation, ❅S#7174h. If this were the name of a place, it is no where else mentioned. Some contend that karkor signifies rest; and the Vulgate renders it requiescebant, rested. This seems the most likely; for it is said (ver. 11) that Gideon “smote the host: for the host was secure.” children. Jg 7:12. fell an hundred, etc. or, an hundred and twenty thousand, every one drawing a sword. Jg 7:22. 20:2, 15, 17, 25, 35, 46. 2 K 3:26. 2 Ch 13:17. 28:6, 8. Is 37:36.`
