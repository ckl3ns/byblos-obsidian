Had his leprosy been over all his body, he would have been clean (Le 13:12, 13). The prophet Elisha commanded Naaman to do what the law of God required for the cleansing of leprosy, and that law required that the leper be sprinkled seven times (Le 14:7). The law did not require anything to be done seven times except the sprinkling; the “shaving, and washing his flesh with water,” was done but once. Thus, the translators of the Septuagint chose to render tabal here alone by baptidzo, for in their mind the word baptidzo referred to ceremonial cleansing, which was accomplished by sprinkling (Nu 19:13n). Fairfield notes that the Hebrew word tabal which occurs here is used sixteen times in the Hebrew Scriptures. Fourteen times it is translated into Greek by the Septuagint translators by bapto, once by moluno (Ge 37:31, to defile or stain), once by baptidzo (Letters on Baptism, p. 54).
