## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`Tiglath-pileser. i.e. lord of the Tigris, ❅S#8407h. Some suppose Tiglath-pileser to be the son of Sardanapalus: but the learned Prideaux makes him the same as Arbaces the Mede, called by Aelian (Hist. Anim. l. xii. c. 12), Thelgamus, and by Castor, Ninus Junior, who, with Belesis, headed the conspiracy against Sardanapalus, and fixed his royal seat at Nineveh, as Belesis, called in Scripture Baladan (Is 39:1), did his at Babylon. He reigned nineteen years, from A.M. 3257 to A.M. 3276. 2 K 16:7, 10. 1 Ch 5:6, 26. 2 Ch 28:20, 21, Tiglath-pilneser. Is 9:1. Ijon. 1 K 15:20. 2 Ch 16:4. Abel-beth-maachah. 2 S 20:14, 15. 1 K 15:20. Janoah. i.e. rest, ❅S#3239h. Jsh 16:6, 7, Janohah. Kedesh. Jsh 19:37. +20:7n. Hazor. Jsh 11:1, 10, 13. 12:19. Jg 4:2. Gilead. Nu 32:1, 40. Dt 3:15. Am 1:3, 13. Galilee. Jsh 20:7n. 1 K 9:11. Is 9:1, 2. Mt 4:15, 16. carried them. 2 K 17:6, 23. Le *26:32, 38, 39. Dt *4:26, 27. *28:25, 64, 65. Is 1:7. 7:20. Thus beginning the threatened captivity of the ten tribes (Young).`
