It properly denotes wash. The washing, it is true, might be performed by bathing; but then it might be performed equally well without it, and where bathing might have been practiced it was not itself sufficient. The force of the word implies some effort made to cleanse, as by rubbing with the hand. And, as President Beecher has well said, bathing fulfilled the command, not because it was a bathing, but solely because it was a washing. Besides, in many cases, as in the washing of hands, the water was poured. At other times it was simply taken out of the vase or vessel by one hand, and applied to the body.
