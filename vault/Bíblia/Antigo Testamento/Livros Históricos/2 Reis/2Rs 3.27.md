## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`eldest son. or, first born. Dt 12:31. 21:15-17. Mi 6:7. offered him. In cases of great extremity, it was customary in various heathen nations, to offer human sacrifices, and even their own children. This was frequent among the Phoenicians, Greeks, Romans, Scythians, Gauls, Africans, and others; and was the natural fruit of a religious system, which had for the objects of its worship cruel and merciless divinities. The king of Moab, in this case, sacrificed his son to obtain the favor of Chemosh, his god, who, being a devil, delighted in blood and murder, and the destruction of mankind. The dearer anything was to them, the more acceptable those idolaters thought the sacrifice, and therefore burnt their children in the fire to their honor. Ge 22:2, 13. Dt 12:31. Jg 11:31, 39. Ps 106:37, 38. Ezk 16:20. Mi 6:7. burnt offering. Jg 11:31, 39. upon the wall. Of the capital city, in the sight of friend and foe (Young). they departed. 1 S 14:36-46. 1 K 20:13, 28, 43. returned. 2 Ch 20:1, 22, 23.`
