## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`Hazor. ƒ66, Ge +9:3. Or rather, Hazar-hadattah. i.e. enclosure of rejoicing: new enclosure, trumpeting of joy, trumpeting anew. Or, as the LXX. Alexandrian, and Vulgate render, Asor nova, “New Hazor,” to distinguish it from the preceding and following (ver. 28) Hazor. Eusebius and Jerome say it was a village in their time, on the eastern confines of Askelon. Kerioth. i.e. cities. ❅S#7152h. Je 48:24, 41. Am 2:2. Or, rather, Kerioth-Hezron, “the cities of Hezron.” Kerioth is supposed to be the birth place of Judas Iscariot, i.e. the man of Kerioth (Young).`
