Kedesh and Hebron were at the two extremities of the land—the former being Galilee, and the latter in Judah, both in mountainous districts, and Shechem was in mount Ephraim, nearly in the center. Bezer was east of Jordan, in the eastern part of the plain opposite Jericho; Ramoth was about the midst of the country of the two tribes and a half, being about the middle of the mountains of Gilead; and Golan, the capital of Gaulonitis, was situated in the tribe of Manasseh, in the land of Bashan. As this institution is considered as a type of Christ (He 6:18-20), some expositors observe a significancy in the names of these cities with application to Him as our Refuge.
