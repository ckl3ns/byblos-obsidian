## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`Manasseh. Jsh 16:9. 1 Ch 7:29. Beth-shean. i.e. house of rest; house of quiet, ❅S#1052h. The Scythopolis of the Greek and Roman writers, was situated in the plain of Jordan, west of that river, 120 furlongs south from Tiberias, according to Josephus, and 600 furlongs north from Jerusalem (2 Mac 12:29). It was the largest city of the Decapolis (see Mt 4:25. Mk 5:20. 7:31), and the only one on that side of Jordan. It is now called Bisan, 8 hours or 24 miles from Tiberias; and described by Dr. Richardson, exclusive of its ruins, as “a collection of miserable hovels, containing 200 inhabitants.” ver. 16. Jg 1:27. 1 S ◐31:10, 12, Beth-shan. 2 S ◐21:12. 1 K 4:12, 12. 1 Ch 7:29. and her towns. Heb. daughters. ƒ155E5, Nu +21:25. Ibleam. i.e. swallowing up a people. Jg 1:27. 2 K 9:27. 1 Ch 6:70, Bileam. Dor. i.e. habitation. Dor, according to Eusebius, was situated on the Mediterranean, nine miles from Caesarea Palestine, towards Carmel. The village of Tortura, four leagues north of Caesarea, is supposed to nearly occupy its site. Jsh 12:23. Jg 1:27. 1 K 4:11. Endor. i.e. fountain of Dor or habitation, ❅S#5874h. 1 S *28:7. Ps 83:10. Taanach. i.e. wandering through; she will afflict thee; humbling thee; sandy, ❅S#8590h. Jsh 12:21. 17:11. Jg 1:27. 5:19. 1 K 4:12. 1 Ch 7:29. Megiddo. i.e. place of multitudes; invading; gathering for cutting; his cutting place; rendezvous. Jg 1:27. 5:19. 1 K 4:12. 9:15. 2 K 9:27. 23:29n, 30. 2 Ch 22:9mg. 35:22. Zc 12:11. Re *16:16.`
