## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`Helkath. i.e. a portion, field, ❅S#2520h. Jsh 21:31. 2 S 2:16. Hali. i.e. ornament, ❅S#2482h. Beten. i.e. belly or womb; valley, ❅S#991h. Probably the same as Bebeten or Batnai, mentioned by Eusebius, eight miles from Ptolemais; and perhaps the Ecbatana which Pliny places not far from Ptolemais. Achsaph. i.e. enchantment; sorcery. Jsh 11:1. 12:20.`
