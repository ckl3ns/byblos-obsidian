## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`the dung gate. Ne 2:13n. 12:31. Rechab. i.e. a rider, chariot. 2 S +4:2. 1 Ch 2:55. Je 35:19. the ruler. ver. 9, 12, 15-18. Beth-haccerem. i.e. house of the vineyard. Je +6:1. Beth-haccerem was a town of Judah, situated on a mountain, between Jerusalem and Tekoa, according to Jerome on Je ch. 6. Dr. Pococke conjectures that the Mountains of the Franks, called also the Mount of Bethulia, from a village of that name near it, west-north-west of Tekoa, is the ancient Beth-haccerem; the position of which seems to agree with the citadel of Herodium, built by Herod, on a moderate-sized hill, sixty furlongs from Jerusalem (Josephus, Ant. l. xiv. c. 13, sec. 9. l. xv. c. 9. sec. 4). Je 6:1.`
