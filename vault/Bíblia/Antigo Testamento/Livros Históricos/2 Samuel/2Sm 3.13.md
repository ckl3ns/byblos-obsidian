## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`that is. Heb. saying. Thou shalt. Ge 43:3. 44:23, 26. except. As Michal was not divorced, but violently separated from David, he had a legal right to demand her, and was justified in receiving her again. It is probable, also, that her marriage with Phaltiel was a force upon her inclinations; and whatever affection he might have for her, it was highly criminal for him to take another man’s wife. David required Michal probably both out of affection for her and to strengthen his interest, by asserting his affinity with the house of Saul. Michal. ver. 20-23. 1 S *18:20-28. 19:11-17. 1 Ch 15:29.`
