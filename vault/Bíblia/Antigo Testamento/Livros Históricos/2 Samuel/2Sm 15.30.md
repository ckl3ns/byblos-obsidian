`the ascent. Zc 14:4. Lk 19:29, 37. 21:37. 22:39. Ac 1:12. mount Olivet. i.e. olive yard, S#2132h. Mount Olivet, so called from its abounding with olive trees, is situated east of Jerusalem, being separated from it only by the valley of Jehoshaphat and the brook Kidron. Josephus says it is five stadia, i.e. 625 geometrical paces, from Jerusalem; and St. Luke (Ac 1:12) says it is a Sabbath day’s journey, or about eight stadia distant, i.e. to the summit. It forms part of a ridge of limestone hills, extending from north to south for about a mile; and it is described as having three, or, according to others, four summits; the central and highest of which overlooks the whole of the city, over whose streets and walls the eye roves as if in the survey of a model. and wept as he went up. Heb. going up and weeping. Ps 42:3-11. 43:1, 2, 5. Lk 19:41. his head covered. This custom was only practiced by persons in great distress or when convicted of great crimes.
