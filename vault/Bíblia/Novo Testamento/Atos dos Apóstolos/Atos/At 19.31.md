## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`the chief. The Asiarchai, Asiarchs, were officers of a religious nature, who presided over the public games instituted in honor of the gods in Asia; by which is meant, in the New Testament, not the quarter of the globe so called, but either Proconsular Asia or Asia Minor, a large country in the form of a peninsula, lying between the Hellespont and Euxine sea on the north, the Mediterranean sea on the south, and the Aegean sea on the west, about 600 miles in length, and 320 in breadth. Paley states “Asia, throughout the Acts of the Apostles and the Epistles of Paul, does not mean the whole of Asia Minor or Anatolia, nor even the whole of the proconsular Asia, but a district in the anterior part of that country, called Lydian Asia, divided from the rest, much as Portugal is from Spain, and of which district Ephesus was the capital” (Horae Paulinae, pp. 47, 48). ver. 10. Ac 16:6. Pr +*16:7. 1 Co 16:19. desiring. Ac 21:12.`
