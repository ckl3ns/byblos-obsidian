`who. See on ver. 15, 30. Ac 19:4. after. ƒ24D, Lk +10:29. Antimereia of the Adverb, Adverb for Noun. The adverbs “after” (lit. behind, Gr. opiso) and “before” (Gr. emprosthen) never refer to time, but to position or grade. Other unmarked instances of this figure are connected with the verbs to become and to be, which with an adverb or adverbial phrase often change the signification of the adverb into that of a noun. See Jn 6:25. 2 S 11:23. Mk 4:10. Ac 5:34. 13:5. Ro 7:3. 16:7. Ep 2:13. 2 Th 2:7. 2 Ti 1:17. whose. Mt 3:11. Mk 1:7. Lk 3:16.`
