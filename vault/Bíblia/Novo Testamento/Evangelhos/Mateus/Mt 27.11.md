Jn *18:33, 39. 19:3, 14, 15, 19, 21. Thou sayest. ƒ108H13, Mt +26:25. By his answer, A.T. Robertson suggests (Word Pictures, vol. 1, p. 225), Jesus confesses that he is the king of the Jews. The Companion Bible margin identifies this as a Hebraism. But Nigel Turner states that the presence of the pronoun in Greek makes the statement emphatic: You have said. Jesus is non-committal, and in essence is suggesting, “Are you putting these words in my mouth? Are you making the claim for me that I am the Messiah? Jesus did not publicly announce his Messiahship before the crucifixion to his enemies. His answer means, “You are saying this, not me.” This form of expression is found at Mt 26:25, 64. 27:11. Mk 15:2. Lk 23:3. There is some manuscript evidence that supports this construction as being the correct reading at Mk 14:62 also (see Nigel Turner’s discussion in his Grammatical Insights, pp. 72-75). Mt *26:25, 64.
