## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`bottles. Jsh 9:4, 13. Jb 32:19. Ps 119:80, 83. Mt 9:17. Lk 5:37, 38. marred. Gr. apollumi, Mt +2:13. new. “New” wine is neos; “new” skins is kainos. The two Greek words for “new,” while generally synonymous, may be distinguished here: neos is new in the sense of brand new, new as opposed to old, referring to age; kainos is new in quality or condition, the sense of fresh, not yet used, but with no reference to age—the new wineskins conceivably could have remained unused for a long time. Perhaps this distinction holds at 2 P 3:13, where “new” heavens and a “new” earth may be understood as new in quality, not in time, for Scripture elsewhere states that the “earth abideth forever” (Ec +*1:4). For neos, ❅S#3501g: rendered (1) new: Mt 9:17, 17. Mk 2:22, 22, 22. Lk 5:37, 37, 38, 39. 1 Co 5:7. He 12:24. (2) new man: Col 3:10. (3) young woman: Ti 2:4. For kainos, ❅S#2537g: Mt 9:17. 13:52. 26:28, 29. 27:60. Mk 1:27. 2:21, 22. 14:24, 25. 16:17. Lk 5:36, 36, 38. 22:20. Jn 13:34. 19:41. Ac 17:19, 21. 1 Co 11:25. 2 Co 3:6. 5:17, 17. Ga 6:15.
