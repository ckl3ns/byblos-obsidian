## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`A.M. 4036. A.D. 32. Herod. Jb 18:11, 12. Ps 73:19. Mt 14:1-12. Mk 6:14-28. Tetrarch. A “tetrarch,” tetrarkees, from tetras, “four,” and arkee, “government,” properly signifies a prince, or ruler over a quarter of any region; and had its origin from Galatia, which was governed by four princes. In the New Testament, however, it denotes a prince, or king, who reigns over the fourth part of a former kingdom. By Herod’s will his kingdom was thus divided among his sons: Archelaus had one-half, consisting of Idumea, Judea, and Samaria; Herod Antipas, one-fourth, consisting of Galilee and Perea; and Philip the remaining fourth, consisting of Batanea, Trachonitis, and Auranitis. he was perplexed. Lk 21:25. Is 22:5. Mi 7:4. Ac 2:12. 5:24. 10:17.`
