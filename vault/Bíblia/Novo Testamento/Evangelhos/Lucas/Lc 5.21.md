Clark notes (Logic, p. 4) this is the figure enthymeme, the conclusion (“Jesus is a blasphemer”) of an argument in which one or both of the premises are unstated or taken for granted. The unstated premises are (1) anyone who can really forgive sins is God (implied in the rhetorical question); (2) Jesus is only a man and is not God (unstated). Therefore, claiming to be God, he is a blasphemer. The argument is valid in form, involving no fallacy, but the second premise is false, rendering the conclusion untrue. Lk 7:49.
