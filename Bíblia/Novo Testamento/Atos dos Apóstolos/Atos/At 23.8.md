## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`Sadducees. Ac 4:1. Mt *22:23, 24. Mk 12:18. Lk 20:27. 1 Co 15:12. spirit. Gr. pneuma, ƒ121A3, Mt +27:50. A “spirit” was the common term for one passed into the unseen state. The Pharisees confessed their belief in “spirits,” carefully distinguished from “angels,” and in opposition to Sadducean infidelity (F. W. Grant, Facts and Theories, p. 75). “Spirit” is taken as ordinarily applying to the spirits of men apart from the body. Ac 7:59. Ec +*12:7. Lk 23:46. He 12:23. 1 P 3:19. confess both. The language of the inspired writer here shows his own consent with this doctrine: “the Pharisees confess (or acknowledge) both.” When I speak of “acknowledging” a thing, I plainly suppose it true, what is acknowledged. And thus in these matters the Pharisaic and the Christian faith are one (F. W. Grant, p. 114, 115). Paul affirms “I am a Pharisee.” Thus Scripture teaches the doctrine of conscious existence after death. Lk +*24:37.`
