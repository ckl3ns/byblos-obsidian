T#1837. Ec *3:13. Ezk +*16:49. neither. ƒ164, 1 S +17:7. ƒ65, Mt +27:19. Bengel on this passage (N.T. Word Studies, vol. 2, p. 502) identifies this as a form of Enthymeme, confirmation of the argument from its contrary. “Supply, But every man eats: therefore let every man labor. Paul does not mean, that such a man should be immediately deprived of food by others; but he proves from the necessity of eating the necessity of laboring. There is a similar Enthymeme at 1 Co 11:6.” More properly, this is the figure Syllogismus, or Omission of the Conclusion, the opposite of the figure Enthymeme, or Omission of the Premise. Pr 19:15.`
