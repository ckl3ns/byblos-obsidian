## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`answered. Jn 5:17, 19. Mk +11:14. Many. ver. 25, 37. Jn 5:19, +20, 36. Mt 11:5. Ac 2:22. *10:38. for which. 1 S 19:4-6. 2 Ch 24:20-22. Ps 35:12. 109:4, 5. Ec 4:4. 1 J 3:12. stone. Stoning was the prescribed punishment for the non-religious crimes of (1) unchastity, including adultery and rape, Le 20:10. Dt 22:21-24. (2) insubordination to constituted authorities (a) parents, Ex 21:15, 17. Le 20:9. Dt 21:18-21. (b) priest or judge, Dt 17:12. Stoning was the prescribed punishment for five capital crimes of a religious nature: (1) sacrifice to idols. Ex 22:20. Le 20:2. Dt 13:6-17. 17:2-7. 2 K 10:18-25. 11:18. 23:5, 20. (2) sorcery. Ex 22:18. Le 20:27. 1 S 28:3, 9. (3) profaning the Sabbath. Ex 31:14, 15. 35:2. Nu 15:32-36. (4) blasphemy. Le 24:10-16, 23. 1 K 21:13. (5) false prophecy. Dt 13:1-5. 18:20. 1 K 18:40. 20:27, 28. When Jesus asked upon what grounds they were about to stone him, their unequivocal answer was for blasphemy, explained in the very next verse, “because that thou, being a man, makest thyself God.” As in the other two cases where the Jews were about to stone Jesus (Jn 5:18. 8:59), Jesus did not deny the correctness of their understanding of his claims. The Jews, therefore, correctly understood Christ’s claim to be deity, and on this basis they charged him with blasphemy. This proves that Jesus himself asserted his own deity and equality with God the Father (see Jn 5:18n). At John 5:18 the assertion that Jesus made himself “equal with God” is John’s own assertion under divine inspiration, not merely his report of the understanding of the Jews.`
