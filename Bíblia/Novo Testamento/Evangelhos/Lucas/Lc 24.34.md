## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`Saying. From Mk 16:13, we learn that the apostles did not believe the testimony even of the two disciples from Emmaus, while it is here asserted they were saying, when they entered the room, “The Lord is risen,” etc. This difficulty is removed by rendering interrogatively, “Has the Lord risen,” etc.? Yet the presence of the word “indeed” indicates that this added evidence of the two disciples was the turning point which convinced the rest that the Lord was risen indeed. the Lord. Lk 7:13. hath appeared. Lk 22:54-62. Mk 16:7. 1 Co 15:5.`
