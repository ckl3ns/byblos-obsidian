Vincent states “The idea is that of confessing Christ out of a state of oneness with him. ‘Abide in me, and being in me, confess me.’ It implies identification of the confessor with the confessed, and thus takes confession out of the category of mere formal or verbal acknowledgment. ‘Not every one that saith unto me, Lord! Lord! shall enter into the kingdom of heaven.’ The true confessor of Christ is one whose faith rests in him. Observe that this gives great force to the corresponding clause, in which Christ places himself in a similar relation with those whom he confesses” (Word Pictures in the New Testament, vol. 1, p. 61).
