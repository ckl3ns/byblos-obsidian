## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`rams’ skins. Ex 26:14. 39:24. He +=2:10. badgers’ skins. Ex 26:14. 39:34. Ps =22:6. Is =53:2, 3. Ezk 16:10. Mk =6:3. Jn =*1:10. Ph =2:6, 7. He =7:26. The original word thus translated occurs out of the Pentateuch only in Ezk 16:10, so that the context throws no light on the subject. Many ancient interpreters understand it to denote a color, though they differ in determining it; jackal, weasel, seal, boar, pardale, fitchet, dolphin, mermaid, dudong, hyaena, walrus, antelope, etc. have all been suggested in addition to badger, which has been objected to as an unclean animal, whose skin would have been unlawful for the tabernacle (Young). shittim wood. Ex 26:15, 26, 37. 27:1. 36:20. Is *41:19. or, acacia wood, which is the largest and most common tree in Arabia, especially near Sinai; whence the name of a place, Nu 25:1; 33:49; Jl 3:18 (Young).`
