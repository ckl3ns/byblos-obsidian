## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`covering. See on Ex 26:14n. rams’ skins dyed red. This was the third covering of the tabernacle. The first and lowermost was made of fine linen, richly embroidered with figures of cherubim, in shades of blue, purple, and scarlet (ver. 8-13). It is reasonable to suppose, that the right side of this curtain was undermost, and so it formed a beautiful ceiling on the inside of the tabernacle. The second covering, which lay over the embroidered one, was made of a sort of mohair (ver. 14-17), and the fourth, or uppermost one, which was to keep the others from the weather, was made of tachash, or badgers’ skins. Ex ◐25:5n.`
