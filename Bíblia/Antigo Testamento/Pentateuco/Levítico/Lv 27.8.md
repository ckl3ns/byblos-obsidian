A rich person might make a vow of some poorer person over whom he had authority, and this poor person might be unable to redeem himself by his poverty, and might claim a reduction thereupon, but the law here declares that the valuation is to be reckoned not by the ability of him who is vowed, but of him who is vowing (Young).`
