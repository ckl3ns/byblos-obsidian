## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`I will make. After being subdued by Nebuchadnezzar, about five years after the destruction of Jerusalem, many of the Edomites, during the Babylonian captivity, being driven from their ancient habitation by the Nabatheans, seized upon the southwestern part of Judea (Strabo, l. xvi); but afterwards they were conquered by Hyrcanus, and reduced to the necessity of embracing the Jewish religion (Josephus, Ant. l. xiii. c. 9. sec. 1); and at last became either incorporated with that nation, or swallowed up and lost among the Nabathean Arabs, so that the very name was abolished and disused about the end of the first century after Christ. Their country is now barren; and their cities, even Bozra and Petra, totally demolished and in ruins (See Prideaux’s Connection, an. 129, Newton on the Prophecies, Dissert. iii., and Burckhardt’s Trav. in Syria, etc.). 2 K 14:7n.
