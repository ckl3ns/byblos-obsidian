Alexander discusses the difficulties in terms of Hebrew syntax of so translating (involving, as it does, an abrupt change from affirmation to interrogation), and concludes “By far the simplest syntax is the common one, which makes the first verb an imperative … and to understand both verbs as conceding an indulgence to those who are addressed.
