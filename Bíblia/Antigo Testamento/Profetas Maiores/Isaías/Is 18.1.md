## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`A.M. cir. 3290. B.C. cir. 714. Woe. Bp. Lowth renders, after Bochart, “Ho! to the land of the winged cymbal;” which he thinks is a periphrasis for the Egyptian sistrum; and consequently, that Egypt, “which borders on the rivers of Cush,” is the country to which the prophecy is addressed. If we translate “shadowing with wings,” it may allude to the multitude of its vessels, whose sails may be represented under the notion of wings. the land. Is 20:3-6. 30:2, 3. 31:1. shadowing. Ru 2:12. Ps 17:8. 36:7. 57:1. 61:4. 63:7. 91:4. Mt 23:37. which. 2 K 19:9. Ezk 30:4, 5. Zp 2:12. 3:10. Ethiopia. 2 Ch 14:9. 16:8.`
