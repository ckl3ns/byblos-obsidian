## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`the kings. 2 K 15:29. *16:9. *17:5, 6, 24. 1 Ch *5:26. Na 2:11, 12. laid waste. 2 K 19:17. Je 51:36. nations. Heb. lands. This is an example of how the Hebrew text in Isaiah varies from the text in 2 Kings. Here, the repetition of lands and land has much perplexed interpreters, as J. A. Alexander notes. Young in his translation follows Gesenius who follows J. D. Michaelis and Augusti in giving the masculine suffix a reflexive sense, translating “laid waste all the land and their land,” indicating that they had destroyed not only other countries but their own, agreeing with the charge against the king of Babylon in Is 14:20, “thou hast destroyed thy land and slain thy people.” But it is better to take this passage in the sense of 2 K 19:17, and regard the differing construction here as a combination of the figures of speech Paronomasia (ƒ140, Ge +4:25) and Metonymy of Adjunct (ƒ121J17, Ge +6:11), where the latter word “land” is put for the people inhabiting the lands. For other instances of this figure see Ge +6:11. countries. ƒ145, Jg +11:40.`
