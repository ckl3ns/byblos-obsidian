A cypher (or secret code, constructed on a system known as Athbash) for Babel, arrived at by numbering the Hebrew alphabet from 1 to 24 forwards and backwards; then exchanging the forward place value of each letter with the letter occupying the position of the equivalent reverse numbering place value.
