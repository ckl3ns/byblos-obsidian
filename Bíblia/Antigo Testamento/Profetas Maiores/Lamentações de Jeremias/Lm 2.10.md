The sign is put for the thing signified, in connected words and phrases. “Sitting on the ground” is put for grief. For other instances of this figure see Jb 2:13. Is 3:26. Sitting on the ground was a posture of mourning and deep distress. Hence the coin struck by Vespasian, on the capture of Jerusalem, has on the obverse side a palm tree, the emblem of Judea, and under it a woman, the emblem of Jerusalem, sitting down, with her elbow on her knee, and her head supported by her hand, with the legend Judaea capta.
