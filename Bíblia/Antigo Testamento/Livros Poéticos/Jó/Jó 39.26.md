## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`the hawk. Netz, Arabic naz, Latin nisus, the hawk, so called from natzah, to “shoot away, fly,” because of the rapidity of its flight; whence Homer (Iliad. l. xv. v. 238) calls it “the swiftest of birds.” It probably comprehends various species of the falcon family, as the ger-falcon, goshhawk, and sparrowhawk. Le 11:16. Dt 14:15. stretch. Is it through thy teaching that the falcon, or any other bird of passage, knows the precise time for taking flight, and the direction in which she is to go to arrive at a warmer climate? SS 2:12. Je 8:7. south. Jb 9:9. Ex 26:18, 35. 27:9.`
