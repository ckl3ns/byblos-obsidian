## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`Iron sharpeneth. ƒ138C, Ge +22:14. As iron or steel will bring a knife to a better edge when properly whetted, so one friend may excite another to reflect deeply, furnish useful hints, and invigorate and improve each other by mutual intercourse. So Horace (Ars Poet. 304), Ergo fungar vice cotis, acutum reddere quae ferrum valet, exors ipsa secandi, “But let me sharpen others as the hone/Gives edge to razors, though itself has none” (Francis). 1 S *13:20, 21. so. ver. *9.
