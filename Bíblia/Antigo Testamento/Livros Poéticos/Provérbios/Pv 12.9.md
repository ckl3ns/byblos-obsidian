## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`He that is, etc. Or, rather, as in the old translation, “He that is despised, and is his own servant, is better than he that boasteth himself and wanteth bread;” with which the versions generally agree. That is, it is better to be in lowness and obscurity, and to support one’s self by manual labor, than to want the necessaries of life, through a foolish vanity, or the pride of birth, which refuses to labor. 2 Th 3:10. 1 Ti 5:8. despised. Pr *13:7. Lk *14:11.`
