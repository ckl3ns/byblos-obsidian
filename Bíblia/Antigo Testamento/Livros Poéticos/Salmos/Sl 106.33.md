## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`Because. Nu *20:10, 11. provoked. ver. 7, 43. 78:17, 40. Jb 17:2. Is 3:8. Ep 4:26. Ja 1:20. spirit. Heb. ruach. ƒ171Q24. Synecdoche of the Part: here, “spirit” is put by synecdoche for the whole person. ✣S#7307h. Ps 77:3, 6. Ezk 21:7. Da 2:1, 3. Ml 2:15, 16. For the other uses of ruach, see Ge 6:3n. he spake. Ps *39:1. *141:3. Ge *30:1. *35:16-18. Jb *2:10. *38:2. 40:4, 5. *42:7, 8. Mt +*12:36. Ja *3:2. As Matthew Poole notes, the psalmist here does not mention what Moses spake because that was fully known from the history, and because he would throw a veil over Moses’s infirmity, imply rather than express his fault. Similar reticence is observed in Chronicles, where David’s sin regarding Bathsheba is omitted from the account. This should inform us as to the need for extreme caution in any application we might make of such a passage as Ja 5:16, “confess your faults one to another,” particularly since the Apostle Paul expressly forbids discussion of what has been done in darkness (Ep 5:11, 12).`
