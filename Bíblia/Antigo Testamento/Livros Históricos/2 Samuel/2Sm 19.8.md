`sat in the gate. ƒ171S7B, Ge +14:7. How prudently and mildly David took the reproof and counsel given him! He shook off his grief, anointed his head, and washed his face, that he might not appear to men to mourn, and then made his appearance at the gate of the city, which was the public place of resort for the hearing of causes and giving judgment as well as a place to ratify special bargains. The people flocked to congratulate him on his and their safety, and that all was well. When we are convinced of a fault, we must amend, though we are told of it by our inferiors in a way which is peculiarly painful to our natural feelings (1 S ◐+*25:17. 2 K +*5:13).
