The brook Kidron, which is but a few paces broad, runs along the valley of Jehoshaphat, east of Jerusalem, to the south-west corner of the city, and then, turning to the south-east, empties itself into the Dead Sea. Like the Ilisus, it is dry at least nine months in the year, being only furnished with water in the winter; and after heavy rains its bed is narrow and deep, which indicates that it must formerly have been the channel for waters which have found some other, probably subterranean course.
