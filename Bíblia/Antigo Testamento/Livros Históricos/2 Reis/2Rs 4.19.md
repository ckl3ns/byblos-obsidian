## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`My head. From this peculiar exclamation, and the season of the year, it is probable he was affected by the coup de soleil, or stroke of the sun, which is by no means uncommon in that climate, and often proves fatal. During the Crusades, the army of King Baldwin IV suffered considerably by this circumstance near Tiberias; and Egmont and Heyman, in the 18th century, found the air about Jericho extremely hot; and say that the year before they were there, it destroyed many persons. Jb 14:1, 2. Je 4:19. head. ƒ84, Ge +6:17.`
