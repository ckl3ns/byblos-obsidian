## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`Babylon. ver. 24. Succoth-benoth. i.e. booths of daughters. Literally, “the tents of the daughters,” ❅S#5524h, only here. 2 K 5:18. 19:37. 21:7. Ge 24:67n. Cuth. i.e. burning, crushing, ❅S#3575h, only here. Cuth is probably the Cush watered by the Gihon, or Araxes, now Aras (Ge 2:13), the ancient country of the Scythians (Herodotus, l. i. c. 201. l. iv. c. 11) where we meet with the Quitians, Coethians, or Coetae, and Cytheans, and the cities of Cotatis, Cetemane, Cythanum, Cyta, Cethena, etc. Nergal. i.e. the lamp rolled, ❅S#5370h, only here. Supposed to denote the solar orb; the emblem of which, according to the Rabbins, was a cock, which we know was sacred to Apollo, “because,” says Helidorus (Aethiop. l. i.), “by a natural sensation of the sun’s revolution to us, they are incited to salute the god.” Ashima. i.e. guiltiness, ❅S#807h, only here. Jarchi says this idol was of the form of a goat.`
