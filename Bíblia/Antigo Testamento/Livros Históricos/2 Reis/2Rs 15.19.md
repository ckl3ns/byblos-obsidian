## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`A.M. 3233. B.C. 771. Pul. i.e. a lord, elephant, or bean, ❅S#6322h. Prideaux supposes that this Pul was the father of the famous Sardanapalus, who was called Sardan with his father’s name annexed, as was frequent in those times, making “Sardanpul;” thus Merodach, king of Babylon, was Merodach-Baladan, because he was the son of Baladan. This Pul began to reign, according to Usher, A.M. 3237, the fifth year of Menahem; and he is supposed to be the same that reigned in Nineveh, when Jonah preached in that city. 1 Ch 5:25, 26. Is ◐*9:1. 66:19. Menahem. 2 K 12:18. 16:8. 17:3, 4. 18:16. Ho 5:13. 8:9, 10. 10:6. to confirm. 2 K 14:5. Je 17:5.`
