Ps 141:6 (overthrown). Je 17:4 (discontinue). sprinkled. Le 6:27. Is 63:3. This terrible mode of punishment appears to have been but rarely used, though we occasionally meet with it during this and subsequent periods. The same punishment, it is well known, obtained among the Romans, who used to throw certain malefactors from the Tarpeian rock (Livy, l.vi. c. 20). This practice obtains among the Moors at Constantia, a town of Barbary (Pitt, pp. 311, 312); and is also of frequent occurrence in Persia (See Sir R. K. Porter’s Travels in Persia, vol. ii. pp. 28-30). and he trode. ver. 26. 2 K 7:20. Is 25:10. La 1:15. Mi 7:10. Ml 4:3. Mt 5:13. He 10:29.`
