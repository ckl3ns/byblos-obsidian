Matthew Poole notes that the diviners cast lots, according to the custom of those ancient and eastern people, what day and what month would be most lucky, for the most effectual and universal extirpation of the Jews; wherein appears both his implacable malice and unwearied diligence in seeking vengeance, and God’s singular providence in disposing the lot to that time, that so the Jews might have sufficient space of time to get the decree reversed, as they did. Scott notes that the event showed the vanity of his oracles or auguries; and illustrates the doctrine of a particular providence over all the affairs of men, and the care of God over his church.
