## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`took Haman. Ezr 6:13. Is 60:14. Lk 1:52. Re 3:9. and arrayed. Est 8:15. 9:3. Ps 30:11. Is 61:3. the street. Pitts (pp. 198, 199) gives a similar account of the mode of honoring a person who turns a Mohammedan, at Algiers: “The apostate is to get on a stately steed, with a rich saddle and fine trappings: he is also richly habited, and has a turban on his head, but nothing of this is to be called his own; only there are given him about two or three yards of broad cloth, which is laid before him on the saddle. The horse, with him on his back, is led all around the city, which he is several hours in doing. The apostate is attended with drums and other music, and twenty or thirty serjeants. They march in order on each side of the horse, with naked swords in their hands. The crier goes before, with a loud voice giving thanks to God for the proselyte that is made.”`
