## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`thirty thousand chariots. The Philistines had no doubt collected troops in this emergency, from all the surrounding nations; but the number of chariots is immensely large beyond any example, and wholly disproportioned to the number of their cavalry. It is probable, therefore, that for sheloshim aileph, “thirty thousand,” we should read shelosh aileph, “three thousand,” with the Syriac and Arabic. Young notes “or charioteers, as in 2 S 10:18; 1 K 20:21; 1 Ch 19:18; or thirty chief chariots; Syr. and Ar. read ‘3000 chariots,’ but even Pharaoh had only 600 (Ex 14:7); Jabin 900 (Jg 4:3); Zerah 300 (2 Ch 14:9).” as the sand. ƒ102B, Ge +13:16.
