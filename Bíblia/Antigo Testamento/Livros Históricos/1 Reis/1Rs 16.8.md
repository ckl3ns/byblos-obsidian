## 🔗 **Referências Cruzadas (NTSK)**

`A.M. 3075. B.C. 929. In the twenty and sixth year. ƒ171T9. Synecdoche of the Part B656. In chronology a part of a time or period is sometimes put for the whole of such period: a part of a year is accounted a whole year. Baasha began to reign in the third year of Asa, and reigned 24 years; yet he died and was succeeded by Elah in the 26th year of Asa; and, in like manner, Elah, who began to reign in the 26th year of Asa, and was killed in the 27th, is said to have reigned two years. Thus it is evident that a part of a year is calculated as a whole year. In the Chinese annals, the whole year in which a king dies is ascribed to his reign, the years of the succeeding king being reckoned only from the beginning of the following year (Jackson’s Chr. Ant. Vol. II. p. 443). 1 K 15:25. Compare the notes on 1 S 30:12n and Jsh 5:11n, where a part of a day is counted as a whole day. Compare 1 K 2:11 (ƒ171T7), where “seven years” is put for seven years and a half.`
